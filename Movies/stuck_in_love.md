## Stuck In Love
*Written and Directed by Josh Boone*

In all honesty I must admit that I watched this movie for the feature of Jennifer Connelly after being mesmerized from the movie Little Children. Unfortunately my reasoning for watching this movie was not the only thing flawed. 

I finished this movie with a taste of resentment and regret, like indulging in a cake that simply is too sweet. Perhaps I missed some of the better qualities of this movie but the morals and ideas represented left me in a quiet distortion. Unlike documentaries, movie are fantasies or at the least fictional. But *Stuck In Love* went all out, and in that sense came across as *highly american* (pardon my french). 

Life is painted as this grand event through which all mistakes resort itself by mere luck. A "happy ending" is underrated in this movie and in no way would I consider this movie to be touching a baseline of what I expect from my own life. Sure, that might be my own fault. As the father at some point states to his son that at the age of eighteen he should have experienced enough to write a lifetime, whereas I, at the age of twenty-six believe most of the experiences are yet to come. In that sense I share more of Murakami's perspective from _"what I talk about when I talk about running"_ where the older he gets the more he discovers about himself (or realizes how much is still undiscovered).

I refuse to romanticize *getting stuck, fucking up and doing stupid shit* with the hope it will all turn out just fine. Yes, generally we (learn to) manage in life. But growing up comes with the painful cost of letting go, which *Stuck in Love* desperately tries to ignore.

3/10
