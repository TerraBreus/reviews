## Vanishing Point

What can I say about this movie? At first I deemed it a little slow. Long shots of the road dissapearing underneath the hood of a dreamcar.

But it grows on you. The long shots become natural, meditative... hypnotizing even. You start to enjoy the roaring of the motor subconsciously. 

Without any prior research done, I can only guess that this movie is a cult classic. The story seems to have multiple layers, however my stoned ass could appreciate the movie only on a superficial level. Still I enjoyed it. 

I enjoyed the absurdity of the movie as well; the whole story is about a two-day-long car chase where the only illegal thing the protagonist is doing is not stopping. Vanishing Point targets certain stereotypes and views in a tangent manner. Political comments are not the main point of the movie, but you can easily see the director commenting on certain (conservative) perspectives.

Knowing the ending, I'm unsure whether I'd want to see the movie again. The suspense and longevity is kept due to not knowing what will happen once Kowalski enters San Fransisco. However, there are clearly more layers to discover that will reveal more about Kowalski since the movie does not reveal much about him at first sight.

7/10
