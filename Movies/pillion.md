# Pillion (2025)
_Directed by Harry Lighton._

Not an easy watch which has rather personal reasons. 

I found it difficult to see such a clear power dynamic in a queer context. I have often alligned my own queerness with a more anarchistic movement for mutual respect to all parties involved. There is a lot of consensual degredation and BDSM that I simply do not understand and is not for me to judge. The movie cleverly addresses my perspective by having Collin's mother saying all of the things I as a viewer thought. Colin's boyfriend responds with a rather dismal and painful truth; _just because it makes you uncomfortable, doesn't mean it is bad_. Ouch...

What made this movie so ichy is how you could feel so much toxicity lying underneath the consensual degredation and roleplaying. The way this story unfolds is absolutely brilliant and as a whole the story is rock-solid. A queer coming-of-age film shining a dark and humourous light on both our desire for political correctness and on toxic masculinity.

7/10 | Watched on 31-03-2026 | Written on 02-04-2026
