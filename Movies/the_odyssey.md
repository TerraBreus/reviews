# The Odyssey (2026)
_Directed by Christopher Nolan_

I know a little about Homerus and recognized some scenes from the mythes themselves. However, I wonder what the purpose of this movie is.
Whereas the original Odyssey meant more to teach (from what I understand), trying to explain that which we do not understand through heroic stories -
Christopher Nolans purpose felt more to entertain with epic warstories about cyclopses, gods and other mythical creatures.

If that is the perspective you have of Homerus, this movie feels a little dissapointed. Like someone making a digestable, mind-numbing action movie of the stories told in the Bible. 

Nonetheless, I must admit it was entertaining. For a movie that is over two and a half hours long, Nolan was able to keep my attention with a lot of fighting scenes and infinite risers. 
The cyclops was one of the coolest, strangely human-like creatures I have seen and it sparked my curiousity how they designed it and got it so human-like on film. 

Job well done there buddy! Just wish it made me think a little more.

6/10
