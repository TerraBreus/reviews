# The history of sound (2025)
*Directed by Oliver Hermanus*

My love for Paul Mescal after seeing *All of us Strangers* and *Aftersun* did not transfer through here unfortunately. While the acting was probably the best element, I could not understand the many strange choices made from the director's perspective. The music for example often felt strangely picked and was cut out in a weird manner at times. For a movie that seems to revolve around music, this was an awfully quiet movie.


The story also did not make the most amount of sense as we never really got to understand what it was that drove the men apart. Some fragments of dialogue mention the war and we are left to assume that the disruption between their love affair was due to this. Also I wish they had made it a little more visible how Lionel (Paul Mescal) was more gifted than the rest. Because to me, this was only clear because of the frequent mentioning how Lionel was so gifted, but its showing as to why was very unclear. 


You had to take their word for it on many elements of this movie. And that did not feel quite right with me.


5/10
