## The Head Hurts but the Heart Knows the Truth
*By Headache*

I came across this album through the recommendation of a colleague during working hours. We ended up listening to it untill customers started complaining. I couldn't care less. 

*Headache* combines the generic with the random creating the absurd. The artificial speaker speaking spoken word over a lofi triphop beat sounds rather washed out, yet it cut right through me. At first I could not hear any of the lyrics as the speakers at work are too bass oriented to hear a single syllable, yet the carefully crafted beats showed me the emotion I was supposed to feel; a bittersweet nostalgia with a mix of melancholy for the joy and pain to come in the future. Hearing the lyrics on my own at a later time allowed me to hear the second and final layer of the simple but powerful combination of *Headache*. The lyrics can be a tid ridiculous with how mundane and random some of the expressions are. At times the lyrics come dangerously close to generic bullshit, but I prefer the term *universal*. I think *Headache* is able to scratch deeply at a feeling we all feel or have felt at some point in our lives; pain. Yet it doesn't see pain as a negative aspect of life, as so many love songs try to point out. *Headache* sees the pain as a meaningful sign of life. Not necessarily a positive one, but a pinching truth that, if accepted, can bring depth, love and colour to life. 

There is a non-linear story in this album that can be discovered when paying close attention but I won't bother with that.

7/10
