## Year of the Slug
By Caroline Rose

Heartfelt acoustic sadness that warms the heart in a slightly self-harming manner. The kind of music you listen to when you want to feel fragile and sad and wallow in it. 

Halfway through the album we get thrown out of the headspace the artists created at first with a strange and annoying song "goddamn train". It would probably be recommended to leave this song out of the album as it has stopped me many times from finishing the album.

Nevertheless the first five songs are good. They have a playful approach towards fragility. Almost ironically teasing difficult topics such as loneliness, refusing adolescence and separation. All in a comforting indie campfire sound.

6/10

Listened to April 2026
