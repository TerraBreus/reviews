## Black Classical Music
_by Yussef Dayes_

Dayes has been on my iPod for a while, but I'm rarely in the mood for it. By accident, I listened to his album while tripping on truffels. This was not a mistake.

Yussef speaks a universal language with his drums, communicating with his fellow instrumentalists. 

A couple days later I tried playing this at my workplace for other people to hear as I was so impressed.
But I must admit it did not hit the same way.
I found songs were too hypnotic, slow and repetive.

Admittedly this is precisely what makes a good listening experience while on psychedelics. But, in comparison to for example Tame Impala's "Currents", one of my favorite psychedelic albums, I found it difficult to find the same appreciation during sobriety.

Nonetheless there is no denying the talent and mastery Dayes has over his drums. 
During the album you start to understand what Dayes is like as a human being with little snippets of his personal life intertwined between the songs which was a nice touch.

Dayes is an incredible musician and seems like a wonderful human being.
But as a (pop) artist he lacks the "oompf" that makes you want to put the album on repeat. 
For one the album is far too long for a quick sit down (over 1 hr long) and second the drums do most of the heavy lifting which removes a lot of the catchiness. In addition I would say that the album also has too many fillers to make this a masterpiece.

6/10

Definitely something to put on as background music or for certain playlists, but not something I would listen to for listening purposes alone (at least while completely sober).
