## HYPERYOUTH
By Joey Valence and Brae

Been waiting on writing this review for a while.

In May 2026 I got to see this duo live. Months before that, when buying tickets, I tried to give HYPERYOUTH a chance. Unfortunately something did not hit right. I couldn't get into it as easily as I had done with NO HANDS.

So the concert came and my only vocabulary of JVB was from NOHANDS. To no surprise I knew very little of the songs played and couldn't enjoy much of the concert. JVB felt a little cringe even at times when wanting us to make friends in the crowd because we all had JVB in common...

So yeah, my hopes and dreams from this band after the show were somewhat diminished.

But then I happened to come across HYPERYOUTH as an mp3 folder convienently downloadable the interwebs and I gave it another shot.

It took a little while to get into, but just as with NOHANDS, once I was sold I didn't want anything else. For about two to three weeks I listened to nothing else except HYPERYOUTH. And I got to say, compared to NOHANDS it is definitely a step up.

JVB still maintains the bangers, but this time with a hinge of emotional awareness. The songs seem to carry a little more baggage. Using BMO to say some lose nostalgic stuff seems to hit that longing for the early 2000's; a time when they were kids and free of worries. This nostalgia and difficulties entering fullblown adulthood seems to be at the center of this album.

The beats and producing are great. Boombap, house, synthwave, dubstep... a mixup of all 2000's genres blended into a simple, sweet and effective club banger. Honestly the producing and beats are what JVB excell at the most I would say. It's just pure gold.

Yes, some of the bars seem a little sleezy (*I'm amazing, and half asian, I'm so good you can call me amasian*, or *Wanna live right* x8) but it's not where the magic happens.

HYPERYOUTH is short and sweet. However the pace of the album is wonderful, with most of the better songs towards the halfway point of the album. It took me a while to get used to but once I was hooked, I was hooked. After a couple of weeks the magic wears off and the songs become a little **too** hyped to listen to casually. Still Joey and Brae have amazing chemistry that is hard not to bop along to, also the beats are amazing so what else do you need?

8/10
