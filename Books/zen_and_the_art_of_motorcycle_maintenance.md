## Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance (DNF)
*Written by Robert M. Pirsig*

Read this book partially for Book Club with Wolf.

Quite a slow read. It would take a lot of conscious effort in order to recall the ideas created from this book.

The things that stood out to me was the type of writing felt boring and tedious. Attempts at romanticising the world were done in a manner as if it were a instructional booklet instructing you on the wonders of the world. Very mechanical, cold and monotone. 

Robert has some interesting ideas for sure, wanting to find the overlap between the romantic and the mechanical. Between the alpha and the beta. Whether he succeeded I find difficult to tell. Perhaps when I'm fifty, abundant in time and brain capacity. As a twenty-six year old, I found Robert Pirsig a strange lad who wants to make the world a better place but cannot acknowledge his shortcomings as a father or acknowledge the needs of his child. 

3/10

Read in January 2026.
