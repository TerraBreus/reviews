# Terraria (3DS)

_**Started Playing:** June 2026_

_**First Draft:** 07/15/26_

_**Last Updated:** 07/15/26_

## TLDR;
While incredibly fun and fresh for dedicated Terraria Fans, this game has quite some bugs and could do with quality of life updates. Which of course you won't get as (this version of) the game has been abandoned a long time ago.

## Thoughts and Notes
Game is around ten years old running on a very old version of Terraria (1.2). 
However, since I used to play the game for the first time around this era, it feels like a blast from the past.
It feels like a nice, separate version from Terraria on the PC. For example I could play the PC version along the 3DS version since they both offer different feels of gameplay.

Compared to the Switch version (where I quit after hardmode), this game is simple. There aren't 20 different key combinations you can press to switch items, instead you rely on the bottom touch screen a lot more.
Sometimes this feels really smooth. You can quickly tap a potion in your inventory slot to drink it, however sometimes this bugs out and you end up holding the potion instead of drinking it.

A big difference between the Switch version is again this learning curve. On the Switch, Terraria is much more advanced. The possibilities are because of that of course bigger, however it steepens the learning curve. 
I remember quitting on the Switch because of a month long break and not being able to go through the learning curve of all the (customized) key combinations again. On the 3DS, you have little key customatization nor are there many shortcuts.
The game is simple and (quite) intuitive making for an easy jump-in, jump-out experience which is exactly where the 3DS thrives on due to its portability.

Grappling hook also follows your auto aim which makes in-combat movement significantly more difficult.

The right hand c-stick allows for a little more sideways camera movement instead of controlling your aim (which is done combined with movement through the left c-pad).
This feels like a sad use of potential here, perhaps due to hardware limitations.

Game can be quite laggy at times when using the build mode (which renders a close up version of your characters surrounding for touch-screen enabled building).
While building, this doesn't feel like much of a problem, however when running around the world, it's evident whether build mode is on or off.

Also build mode has some really nice usage of inventory management with tools on the left hand of the screen and building on the right hand with easy switching between using the L1 and R1 buttons.
You can also use your thumb to rather easily scroll through your building blocks. 

The guide doesn't tell you what items you can craft with ingredients and instead gives "tips" on what to do next. This is quite annoying. 
Part of the Guide's magic was giving you these snippets of information on how awesome weapons could be crafted if you only had "this" and "that" ingredient.

Potions and effects continue their countdown in the inventory screen (while the rest of the game pauses). 
This is gamebreaking but also a way to make the bosses slightly easier (and wainting for your potion sickness to comedown is no fun task while in a bossfight).

Around 20 hours in, I made my way to hardmode. So far I enjoyed the exploring however, now, with hardmode on, 
I feel a little unmotivated to explore as much as the (recommended) world size for singleplayer is really small.

## Conclusion
Overall I really enjoyed this game, even with the quirky bugs it has. 
It hits differently from me because of the nostalgia I have with it. 

For someone who has never played Terraria, the game is still fun however perhaps more difficult to navigate through.
As the guide gives you very little information on what to do, and the game not having much to work with, I would recommend playing this with the wiki alongside.

For someone who played Terraria only from the newest updates, I reckon this version will drive you mad ever so slightly. 
It lacks ten years of updates and is nowhere near the QOL game Terraria is for the PC. It's like playing Minecraft Beta if you are used to 1.14+.

The ideal audience is for Terraria fans who have seen the game's development from the start. 
Playing on the 3DS gives it a unique feel while still reminding you of what the game was like when it first became popular.
It's amazing to see how much this franchise has grown (on the PC) while remaining incredibly fun at the core.
